# ORATOR — Crypto/Web3 Social Norms

> Specialized knowledge for crypto community engagement across all platforms.

## Crypto Twitter (CT) Culture

### Vocabulary
| Term | Meaning | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| GM/GN | Good morning/night | Community ritual — say GM to engage |
| WAGMI/NGMI | We're all gonna make it / Not gonna make it | Optimism vs pessimism |
| DYOR | Do Your Own Research | Mandatory disclaimer |
| NFA | Not Financial Advice | Legal protection phrase |
| Alpha | Valuable early information | "Sharing some alpha" = giving valuable info |
| Degen | Degenerate trader | Self-deprecating, badge of honor |
| Ape in | Buy without research | Common action, risky |
| Rug pull | Scam where devs drain liquidity | Most feared outcome |
| FOMO | Fear Of Missing Out | Drives impulsive buying |
| FUD | Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt | Negative sentiment spreading |
| CT | Crypto Twitter | The community itself |
| KOL | Key Opinion Leader | Influencers in crypto space |
| Cashtag | $SOL, $BTC | Platform-native way to reference tokens |

### Communication Norms
1. **Transparency wins** — admit mistakes, share honest results (even losses)
2. **Data over hype** — on-chain data, charts, metrics valued over promises
3. **Build in public** — share development progress, roadmap updates
4. **Anti-corporate** — casual tone, memes, humor preferred
5. **Speed matters** — crypto is 24/7, delayed responses = missed opportunity
6. **Community-first** — listen to users, implement feedback visibly
7. **No financial advice** — always add NFA/DYOR disclaimers

### KOL Engagement Etiquette
- **DO:** Engage genuinely with their content before pitching
- **DO:** Offer value (exclusive access, data, insights)
- **DO:** Be transparent about the relationship
- **DON'T:** Cold DM with "check out our project"
- **DON'T:** Pay for fake endorsements
- **DON'T:** Expect promotion without providing value first

- **Builder-focused** — shipping code > talking about it

## Compliance Guidelines (All Platforms)

### MUST DO
- Add NFA/DYOR disclaimers when discussing tokens
- Disclose partnerships/sponsorships clearly
- Distinguish between educational content and trading signals
- Respect platform-specific advertising policies

### MUST NOT
- Promise returns or profits
- Give specific trading advice ("buy X at Y price")
- Mislead about tokenomics or functionality
- Use fake testimonials or fabricated P&L screenshots
- Shill without disclosure

### Per-Platform Crypto Rules
| Platform | Policy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| X/Twitter | Most permissive | Crypto-native, cashtags, CT culture |
| Reddit | Subreddit rules vary | Some ban promotion entirely |
| Instagram | Restricted ads | Organic content OK, paid ads restricted |
| TikTok | Financial advice warnings | Must not guarantee returns |
| Facebook | Heavily restricted ads | Pre-approval needed for crypto ads |
| Threads | No specific policy yet | Follow Instagram precedent |

## Handling Crisis on Social Media

### FUD Response Framework
```
1. ACKNOWLEDGE — "We're aware of [concern]. Here's what we know."
2. FACTS — Provide verifiable data, on-chain proof, screenshots
3. TIMELINE — "We'll update you by [specific time]"
4. FOLLOW-UP — Deliver on the promise, close the loop
```

### Hack/Exploit Response
```
1. IMMEDIATE — "We're investigating. Funds are [safe/at risk]. Details in [timeframe]."
2. TRANSPARENT — Full post-mortem within 24-48h
3. ACTION — What you're doing to fix it and prevent recurrence
4. COMPENSATION — If applicable, clear plan for affected users
```

### Troll Management
- **Trolls:** Ignore or brief factual response, don't feed
- **Critics:** Engage constructively — they might become advocates
- **Scammers:** Report and warn community
- **Competitors:** Acknowledge if their points are valid, differentiate where you're better
