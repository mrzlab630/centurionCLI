# QUAESTOR — Market Cycles Reference

> Frameworks for identifying market phases, predicting transitions, and timing entries/exits.

## 1. Wyckoff Method

Richard Wyckoff (1930s) — price moves through 4 phases driven by institutional "Composite Man."

### Accumulation Phase
```
         ┌── PS ── SC ── AR ── ST ──── SPRING ── SOS ── LPS ──┐
Price     │  ↓↓    ↓↓↓   ↑↑    ↓      ↓↓↓       ↑↑↑    ↓↑   │→ MARKUP
Volume    │  ↑↑    ↑↑↑   ↑     ↓      ↑↑        ↑↑↑    ↓    │
```
| Event | Name | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| **PS** | Preliminary Support | First sign of buying interest after downtrend |
| **SC** | Selling Climax | Panic selling, high volume, wide spread — smart money absorbs |
| **AR** | Automatic Rally | Bounce after SC, short covering + early buyers |
| **ST** | Secondary Test | Retest of SC area on lower volume — confirms bottom |
| **SPRING** | Spring/Shakeout | False breakdown below range — trap for bears, last chance accumulation |
| **SOS** | Sign of Strength | Strong move up on increased volume — confirms accumulation complete |
| **LPS** | Last Point of Support | Pullback after SOS on low volume — final entry point |

### Distribution Phase (Mirror of Accumulation)
| Event | Name | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| **PSY** | Preliminary Supply | First sign of selling interest after uptrend |
| **BC** | Buying Climax | Euphoric buying, high volume — smart money distributes |
| **AR** | Automatic Reaction | Drop after BC — first sell-off |
| **ST** | Secondary Test | Retest of BC area on lower volume — confirms top |
| **UTAD** | Upthrust After Distribution | False breakout above range — trap for bulls |
| **SOW** | Sign of Weakness | Drop below range on volume — confirms distribution |
| **LPSY** | Last Point of Supply | Weak bounce — final exit point |

### Markup & Markdown
- **Markup:** Trending up after accumulation. Higher highs, higher lows. Volume confirms moves.
- **Markdown:** Trending down after distribution. Lower highs, lower lows. Bounces on decreasing volume.

### Wyckoff Laws
1. **Supply & Demand** — price moves where supply/demand imbalance exists
2. **Cause & Effect** — accumulation range width → markup magnitude (point & figure count)
3. **Effort vs Result** — volume (effort) should match price movement (result). Divergence = reversal signal.

## 2. Elliott Wave Theory

Ralph Elliott (1938) — markets move in fractal wave patterns driven by crowd psychology.

### Basic Structure
```
        5
       /\      Impulse (5 waves WITH trend)
    3 /  \
   /\/    \    B
  / 4      \  /\     Corrective (3 waves AGAINST trend)
 /          \/ A\
1           /    \
           /      C
```

### Rules (CANNOT be broken)
1. Wave 2 NEVER retraces more than 100% of Wave 1
2. Wave 3 is NEVER the shortest of waves 1, 3, 5
3. Wave 4 NEVER enters the price territory of Wave 1

### Guidelines (Usually hold)
- Wave 3 is typically the longest and strongest (1.618× Wave 1)
- Wave 2 typically retraces 50-61.8% of Wave 1
- Wave 4 typically retraces 38.2% of Wave 3
- Waves 2 and 4 alternate in form (if 2 is sharp, 4 is flat, and vice versa)

### Fibonacci Relationships
| Ratio | Application |
|---|---|
| **0.382** | Common W4 retracement, W2 shallow retracement |
| **0.500** | Common W2 retracement |
| **0.618** | Common W2 deep retracement, WB retracement |
| **1.000** | W5 = W1 (common), WC = WA (common) |
| **1.618** | W3 extension (most common), W5 extension |
| **2.618** | Extended W3 in strong trends |

### Wave Degrees (Fractal)
Grand Supercycle > Supercycle > Cycle > Primary > Intermediate > Minor > Minute > Minuette > Sub-Minuette

Each wave contains sub-waves of the next smaller degree. A 5-wave impulse contains 21 sub-waves (5-3-5-3-5).

### Crypto-Specific Notes
- Crypto tends toward extended 3rd waves (strong momentum)
- 5th waves often produce blow-off tops in alt markets
- Corrective waves in crypto are often deeper (61.8-78.6%) due to higher volatility
- Meme coins rarely follow clean Elliott patterns — too manipulation-driven

## 3. Bitcoin Halving Cycles

~4-year cycle driven by BTC supply reduction events.

### Historical Halvings
| Halving | Date | Block Reward | Price at Halving | Cycle Peak | Peak Multiple |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1st** | Nov 2012 | 50→25 BTC | ~$12 | ~$1,100 (Dec 2013) | ~92× |
| **2nd** | Jul 2016 | 25→12.5 BTC | ~$650 | ~$19,700 (Dec 2017) | ~30× |
| **3rd** | May 2020 | 12.5→6.25 BTC | ~$8,700 | ~$69,000 (Nov 2021) | ~8× |
| **4th** | Apr 2024 | 6.25→3.125 BTC | ~$64,000 | TBD | TBD |

### Cycle Phases (Typical)
```
Halving → 6-12 months sideways → Parabolic rally → Blow-off top → 80% crash → Bear market → Accumulation → Next halving
```

| Phase | Duration | Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| **Post-Halving Accumulation** | 3-6 months | Supply shock builds, price range-bound |
| **Early Bull** | 6-12 months | Steady appreciation, smart money enters |
| **Parabolic Phase** | 2-4 months | Exponential rise, retail FOMO, media frenzy |
| **Blow-Off Top** | Days to weeks | Final spike, maximum euphoria |
| **Crash** | 1-3 months | 50-80% drawdown from peak |
| **Bear Market** | 12-18 months | Grinding down, capitulation, builder activity |
| **Accumulation** | 6-12 months | Bottom formation, smart money re-enters |

### Diminishing Returns Pattern
Each cycle's peak multiple is roughly 1/3 to 1/4 of the previous. The market is maturing. Expecting 100× from halving is unrealistic for BTC at current market cap.

## 4. Altcoin Rotation Cycle

Capital flows through crypto sectors in a predictable pattern within each bull cycle.

### Rotation Order (Typical)
```
BTC rally → ETH/Majors catch-up → Mid-cap alts → Small-cap alts → Meme coins → NFTs/Speculative → CRASH
     ↑                                                                                                    ↓
     └──────────────────────── Bear market / Accumulation ←────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Key Metrics
| Metric | Signal |
|---|---|
| **BTC Dominance rising** | Risk-off, capital flowing to safety, alts underperform |
| **BTC Dominance falling** | Risk-on, "alt season," capital flowing to speculation |
| **ETH/BTC rising** | Alt season beginning, Ethereum leading |
| **ETH/BTC falling** | Alt season ending or BTC-only rally |
| **Total3 rising faster than Total** | Small caps outperforming (late-cycle euphoria) |

### Solana Ecosystem Specifics
- SOL tends to lead in meme coin cycles (fast/cheap TX = meme-friendly)
- Pump.fun launches = indicator of speculative peak
- New token creation rate = sentiment proxy (high creation = euphoria, low = bear)

## 5. On-Chain Cycle Indicators

### Top/Bottom Indicators
| Indicator | What It Measures | Cycle Top Signal | Cycle Bottom Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MVRV Z-Score** | Market Value vs Realized Value (deviation) | Z > 7 | Z < 0 |
| **SOPR** | Profit/Loss of spent outputs | Sustained > 1.05 | Sustained < 0.95 |
| **Pi Cycle Top** | 111-DMA crosses 2×350-DMA | Cross occurs | N/A (top only) |
| **Puell Multiple** | Daily issuance value / 365-day MA | > 4 | < 0.5 |
| **Reserve Risk** | Price vs long-term holder conviction | > 0.02 | < 0.0025 |
| **NUPL** | Net Unrealized Profit/Loss | > 0.75 (Euphoria) | < 0 (Capitulation) |
| **Thermocap Ratio** | Market cap / cumulative security spend | > 32 | < 4 |
| **Realized Price** | Average acquisition cost of all supply | Price well above | Price approaches |
| **RHODL Ratio** | 1-week vs 1-2 year Realized Cap | > 50,000 | < 350 |
| **STH SOPR** | Short-term holder SOPR | Sustained > 1.02 | Sustained < 0.97 |

### On-Chain for Solana (SPL tokens)
Standard MVRV/SOPR are Bitcoin-native (UTXO model). For Solana:
- **Holder distribution** — track % of supply in top 10/50/100 wallets
- **New wallet creation rate** — proxy for adoption
- **DEX volume** — Raydium/Orca/Jupiter aggregate
- **Staking ratio** — high staking = low liquid supply = bullish
- **Validator count** — network health metric

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Accumulation** | Range-bound, higher lows | Low, declining | Exchange outflow, whale buying | Fear, disbelief |
